Constrained optimisation: diet

15 Jul 2016
by Jesper Wohlert

As you are a computer scientist you have already scraped every cooking website on the face of planet for recipes along with the nutrient contents of these recipes. This has left you with a database of several thousand recipes, and you are looking to find a subset of recipes that satisifies the advice of your dietitian. This is a herculean task and you are already hungry for breakfast.

Luckily, this post describes exactly how you can solve this dilemma in sub-exponential time. Three different methods will be presented to highlight how the problem can be solved. The main problem will stay the same for each solution, but additional parameters will be presented in order to show the strengths of each technique.

  • Greedy optimisation: Knapsack
  • Convex optimisation: Linear programming
  • Local optimisation: Simulated annealing

Knapsack

It seems intuitive to model the problem using a 0-1 knapsack. After all, what we are looking to achieve is to put recipes into a hypothetical knapsack until we cannot fit anymore recipes. In this case our weight restriction could be in the total number of calories.
