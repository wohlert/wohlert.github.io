The family of elliptical distributions are characterised by two independent vectors, $\mu$ and $\tau$ that are
the * and radial components that define a sample. Additionally, they all share some generator

Characteristic generator for $X \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I_d)$ is given by the product

$$t = (t_1, \dots, t_d) \mapsto \prod_{i=1}^{d} \exp \left ( - \frac{t_i^2}{2} \right ) = \exp \left ( - \frac{t't}{2} \right )$$

Therefore $\phi(s) = \exp(-s/2)$

All centered elliptical distributions can be characterised as a transformation from the spherical distribution to the
ellipse using some linear transformation. This transformation is specified by the eigenvalues and eigenvectors that
specify the magnitudes and directions of this transformation.
